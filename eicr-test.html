What is an EICR test?

An Electrical Installation Condition Report test is an inspection and test of the fixed wiring in a property. It checks whether the electrical installation is safe to continue using and whether there are any faults, deterioration, poor workmanship or missing protective measures that need attention.

What happens during an EICR test?

We start with a visual inspection of the consumer unit, accessories, visible wiring, earthing and bonding. After that, we carry out testing on the circuits where appropriate. The aim is to assess the condition of the installation properly rather than just giving it a quick glance and hoping for the best.

Visual inspection

We check the consumer unit, sockets, switches, lights, visible cable routes, meter cupboard, earthing arrangements and bonding to gas or water where present.

Circuit testing

We test the circuits from the fuseboard to confirm they are wired correctly, protected properly and suitable for continued use. This can include checks on continuity, insulation, polarity and protective devices depending on the installation.

Power interruptions

The power usually needs to be turned off briefly for parts of the test. We keep disruption to a minimum, but it is still sensible to plan around alarms, IT equipment and anything else that may not enjoy being cut off for a bit.

Report issued by email

Once the inspection is complete, we issue the EICR report digitally to your email address. That keeps things simple and gives you the paperwork ready to forward to tenants, buyers, agents or whoever else wants it.

Pass or fail — what does that actually mean?

The report outcome will be either Satisfactory or Unsatisfactory. If the installation passes, that means no dangerous or potentially dangerous issues were identified at the time of inspection. If it fails, the report will explain what faults were found and how serious they are.

What happens if the EICR passes?

If the report is satisfactory, you keep the certificate on file and use it where needed — for landlords, buyers, insurers, letting agents or just your own records. In plain English: good news, paperwork done, move on with your life.

What happens if the EICR fails?

If the report is unsatisfactory, that usually means a C1, C2 or FI observation has been found. We explain what the issue is, what the code means, and can provide a remedial quote if you want the faults corrected. Once the relevant work is done, affected circuits can then be retested.

How long does an EICR test take?

Most standard homes take around 2 to 3 hours. Larger homes, older properties, homes with more circuits, garages, outbuildings, multiple boards or poor access may take longer.

What can cause an EICR to fail?

An EICR is unsatisfactory if it contains any C1, C2 or FI observations. Typical causes include no RCD protection where required, missing bonding, damaged accessories, exposed live parts, reversed polarity, no CPC at certain fittings, loose connections or anything that needs further investigation before safety can be confirmed.

Common faults that show up on EICR testing

Examples include metal light fittings with no earth, bathroom lighting with no RCD protection where required, outside sockets without proper protection, old consumer units, no earth bonding to gas or water, incorrect breaker sizes, damaged sockets or switches, and exposed cable entries at the board.

EICR observation codes and what they mean

The report uses standard codes so you know what matters and what can wait:

C1 – Danger Present Immediate risk of injury. We make safe where possible. This fails the report.
C2 – Potentially Dangerous Unsafe if a fault occurs. Urgent remedial work required. This fails the report.
C3 – Improvement Recommended Below current standards but not unsafe by itself. This does not fail the report on its own.
FI – Further Investigation Something needs more investigation before the installation can be fully assessed. This fails the report until resolved.

How to prepare for an EICR test

Please make sure we can access the consumer unit, meter cupboard and key areas of the property. If you have alarms, server equipment, freezer stock or anything else sensitive to power interruption, mention it before the visit so the test can be planned properly.

EICR Test FAQs

Does an EICR test turn the power off?

Yes, usually for short periods. Some parts of the testing need the circuits isolated so they can be checked safely and properly.

Can tenants stay in during the test?

Yes, usually, but they should know the power may be off briefly and access to the consumer unit and rooms may be needed.

Does a C3 fail an EICR?

No. A C3 is an improvement recommendation only. The report becomes unsatisfactory if there is a C1, C2 or FI observation.

What if access is limited on the day?

Restricted access can limit the scope of inspection and may result in further investigation being noted. It is always better to say in advance if parts of the installation may not be accessible.

How quickly will I get my report?

Most residential reports are delivered the same day or next working day, depending on the size and condition of the installation.
